Your Almanac

Did you know, the Old Farmer’s Almanac, has been continuously published in the United States since 1792.

The historical data, handed down for generations,is priceless for farmers, and hobby gardeners alike. interested in growing and living off the land.

It's a process, that starts with the first step, and never ends. You can record events, your plants, what works, and what doesn't.

Events

Garden events are cyclic in nature, which means that you can be sure what happens today, will more than likely happen again in the future.

Recording events is your historical record, a foundation of experience, a reference to what worked and what you could do different.

Knowing when events are likely to occur helps you to prepare, to be ready and to make the most of the seasonal changes.

Plants

Preparing, nourishing and enjoying the fruits of your labour is easy when you know how. Knowing what, and when to feed your plants, and how to care for them in different conditions comes from experience.

Build a data base of information for future reference. Little by little, edit your notes, on how to care for your garden.

Farmers relied on this for generations, the wisdom is tried and true.
